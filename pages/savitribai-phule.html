Savitribai Phule(1831-1897)

Savitribai Phule was born on 3 January 1831 Naigaon, Bombay Presidency, British India(present-day Maharashtra, India). She was a Dalit woman and a pioneer of feminism in India. She was also the countryâ€™s first female teacher who went on to set up 17 more schools that imparted education to women of all castes.
She worked alongside her husband and other women, to abolish caste and gender discrimination. She also set up the 'Balhatya Pratibandhak Griha' to prevent female infanticide, and campaigned against the killing of widows and pregnant rape victims. Savitribai Phule is highly revered by advocates of the feminist movement in India today.
Savitribai Phule was also an author and poet. She published Kavya Phule in 1854 and Bavan Kashi Subodh Ratnakar in 1892, and also a poem entitled "Go, Get Education" in which she encouraged those who are oppressed to free themselves by obtaining an education. As a result of her experience and work, she became an ardent feminist. She established the Mahila Seva Mandal to raise awareness for issues concerning women's rights.
